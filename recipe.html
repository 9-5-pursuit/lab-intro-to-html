Oyakodon (Japanese Chicken and Egg Rice Bowl) Recipe

Description

Oyakodon is a classic comfort food of Japanese home cooking. Chicken, onion, and eggs are simmered in a dashi-based sauce and then poured over a bowl of steamed rice. Simple, delicious, and utterly comforting.

Ingredients

  • 10" Frying Pan or Skillet with a Lid
  • Measuring cup or set of measuring cups
  • Wooden spoon, or Chopsticks if you can
  • 1 cup (240ml) homemade or instant dashi(1 pinch of instant dashi and 1 cup water)
  • 2 tablespoons (30ml) dry sake
  • 1 tablespoon (15ml) soy sauce, add more for a stronger taste
  • 1 tablespoon (15g) sugar, add more for a stronger taste
  • 1 large onion (about 6 ounces; 170g), thinly sliced
  • 12 ounces (340g) boneless, skinless chicken thighs or breast, thinly sliced
  • 2 large eggs, lightly beaten

Directions:

  1. Combine dashi, sake, soy sauce, and sugar in a 10-inch skillet and bring to a simmer over high heat. Adjust heat to maintain a strong simmer. Stir in onion and cook, stirring occasionally, until onion is half tender, about 5 minutes. Add chicken pieces and cook, stirring and turning chicken occasionally, until chicken is cooked through and broth has reduced by about half, 5 to 7 minutes for chicken thighs or 3 to 4 minutes for chicken breast.Then add more soy sauce or sugar as desired if the sauce is not already to your taste. The sauce should have a balanced sweet-and-salty flavor.
  2. Reduce heat to a bare simmer. Pour beaten eggs into skillet in a thin, steady stream, holding chopsticks over edge of bowl to help distribute eggs evenly. Cover and cook until eggs are cooked to desired doneness, about 1 minute for runny eggs or 3 minutes for medium-firm.
  3. To Serve: Transfer hot rice to a single large bowl or 2 individual serving bowls. Top with egg and chicken mixture, pouring out any excess sauce from the pan over rice. Serve immediately.
Recipe simplified from: J. Kenji Lopez-Alt on Serious Eats.
